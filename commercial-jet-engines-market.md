<p><p>The commercial jet engine market includes three primary types: turbojet, turbofan, and turboprop engines. Turbojet engines, characterized by their high-speed performance, are suited for military aircraft and some older commercial jets. Turbofan engines, known for their efficiency and quieter operation, dominate commercial aviation, ideal for medium to long-haul flights. Turboprop engines, which utilize propellers driven by a gas turbine, are efficient for short-haul flights and regional air travel, combining performance with fuel economy. Each type serves distinct operational needs.</p></p>
