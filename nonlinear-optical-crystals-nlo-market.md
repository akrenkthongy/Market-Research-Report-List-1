<p><p>Nonlinear optical crystals (NLO) are materials that exhibit nonlinear optical properties, enabling them to manipulate light in various applications such as frequency conversion and optical switching. Key types in the market include KTP (Potassium Titanyl Phosphate), widely used for frequency doubling; BBO (Beta Barium Borate), known for its high damage threshold; LBO (Lithium Triborate) for its broad transparency range; and CLBO (Calcium Lithium Borate) for efficient second-harmonic generation. Other types like DKDP (Deuterated Potassium Dihydrogen Phosphate), ADP (Ammonium Dihydrogen Phosphate), and KDP (Potassium Dihydrogen Phosphate) also play significant roles, alongside various emerging crystals in this sector.</p></p>
