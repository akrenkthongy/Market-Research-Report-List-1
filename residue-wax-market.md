<p><p>Residue wax is a byproduct derived from the refining of petroleum, primarily generated during the process of producing lubricating oils and other hydrocarbon products. It is characterized by its solid or semi-solid form and consists of a mixture of hydrocarbons, primarily long-chain alkanes. Residue wax finds applications in various industries, including packaging, cosmetics, personal care products, and candles, owing to its desirable properties such as flexibility, low acidity, and resistance to moisture.</p><p>The Residue Wax Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.8% during the forecast period, driven by the increasing demand for high-quality wax products across different sectors. Additionally, innovations in processing and application technologies are enhancing the versatility of residue wax, opening new avenues for growth. The packaging industry's expansion, particularly in food and consumer goods, is also a significant factor contributing to the market's upward trajectory, as residue wax is utilized for coatings and sealants that enhance product shelf life and safety.</p></p>
